Mapo Tofu

Description

Mapo tofu is a famous and popular dish originating from the Sichuan province of China. It consists of tofu cooked in a spicy sauce along with ground beef. Various versions and adaptations can be found, from Japanese to Korean to vegetarian, but the authentic version combines conventional spiciness with the characteristic numbing spiciness flavor of Sichuan cuisine. I was born in Sichuan China and grew up eating this dish, and it is one of my favorite meals!

Ingredients

  • soft tofu
  • sichuan peppercorns
  • minced beef or pork (or vegetarian substitute)
  • garlic
  • sichuan chili bean paste ('pi xian dou ban jiang')
  • chili powder
  • stock
  • canola oil (cai zi you/rapeseed oil is best if you can find it)
  • rice wine
  • soy sauce
  • sugar
  • dark chinese vinegar
  • cornstarch slurry
  • sesame oil
  • green onion

Steps

  1. Toast and grind your Sichuan peppercorns.
  2. Cut and simmer your tofu.
  3. Fry your protein
  4. Fry your chili bean paste
  5. Fry your minced garlic and chili powder
  6. Add stock, season, and simmer
  7. Drain your tofu and add it to your pot, simmer
  8. Season the sauce, then thicken it with your slurry, add the
    9. peppercorn powder and dark vinegar
  9. Stir in sesame oil, garnish with green onion slices
